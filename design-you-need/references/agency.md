### Color Craft Insights
- **[9]** The color palette is driven by the 3D scene's lighting rig, not arbitrary brand colors — this creates cohesion between UI and content that most sites lack
- **[9]** The green palette avoids the typical agency blue/purple trap — the muted sage-to-forest range feels organic and premium, and the salmon pink badge is the perfect complementary surprise that breaks monotony
- **[9]** The gradient isn't a simple linear — it mimics real atmospheric scattering with a concentrated glow zone in the mid-section, creating a natural horizon line that anchors the composition
- **[9]** The color palette shifts per portfolio piece — dark teal for automotive, soft lavender for crypto, warm cream for e-commerce — showing intentional art direction per project rather than one locked palette
- **[9]** The warm cream (#F7F6EC) instead of pure white is the quiet genius — it makes the photography feel cohesive and the red feel warmer, while the blue-to-cream transition mimics golden hour sky photography that matches the lifestyle imagery
- **[9]** The warm radial glow behind the cards simulates stage lighting, making the 3D carousel feel like objects in a physical showroom rather than flat UI
- **[8]** The complete absence of color forces the architectural renders to be the sole source of visual richness — everything else is subordinate
- **[8]** The background isn't pure cream — it has a peachy-pink warmth that makes the interior photography feel native rather than pasted on. Every photo's color temperature was curated to match.
- **[8]** The dark card uses what appears to be a subtle backdrop-filter or rgba dark fill to float over the render while maintaining image visibility — layered depth without gradients
- **[8]** The green-undertone black (#060E07) is deliberate — it harmonizes with the neon green accent creating a cohesive tonal family that pure #000 wouldn't achieve

## Design Recipes

### Score 9/10 — dark-luxury — Cinematic 3D visualization and real-time rendering technolog

**Design Recipe:** Full-bleed dark canvas (#040A11) with cinematic 3D scene as hero. Nav: transparent overlay, logo left, centered links in 16px light-weight geometric sans, outline button right. Typography: ultra-wide letter-spacing (0.3em) uppercase headings in 72px weight-200 Eurostile/Microgramma variant, silver-white (#C3C5D1). Accent color (#10D94E neon green) exists ONLY in the 3D scene lighting — never in UI elements. Bottom-anchored title block with subtitle in 18px uppercase tracking. Pagination counter bottom-right. Everything breathes — 40px+ nav padding, dramatic negative space around text.

**Dev Recipe:** React + @react-three/fiber + @react-three/drei for 3D scene, GSAP ScrollTrigger for scroll-driven camera animation, post-processing with UnrealBloomPass for neon glow. CSS: minimal overlay system with position:fixed, z-index layering, backdrop-filter:none to keep transparency clean over WebGL canvas.

**Core Lesson:** Let the 3D content BE the design — restrain the UI to transparent overlays with ultra-light typography so the visual spectacle speaks for itself

**Steal These:**
- Screen-within-3D-scene as meta-commentary on the product's capability
- Color accent existing only in 3D lighting, never in UI chrome
- Ultra-wide letter-spacing (0.3em+) on thin-weight uppercase for luxury feel
