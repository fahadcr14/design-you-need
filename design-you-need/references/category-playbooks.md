## E Commerce (67 projects, avg: 7.1)

**Top Aesthetics:** warm-minimal (4), editorial (3), dark-luxury (1)

### Best Design Recipes
1. Use #E6EAEB cool off-white background with #221B15 warm-black text. Set headlines in a high-contrast Didone serif (Playfair Display/Freight Display) at 72-80px regular weight with tight tracking (-0.02em). Body in 14px geometric sans (Inter/Helvetica Neue). Add thin geometric diamond overlays in #B5C8BC at 30% opacity behind hero image. Bottom ticker bar with brand values connected by horizontal rules. Zero shadows, zero gradients, zero bright colors — let product photography be the only saturation.

2. Use #030404 background with #FEFEFE text and a single deep red (#C41E1E at 60% opacity) accent ONLY for labels. Set hero type at 15vw in a bold geometric grotesk (Neue Montreal Bold), layer a full-bleed action photo ON TOP of the type using z-index. All section labels: 11px uppercase, 0.12em letter-spacing, red. Body text in 15px regular weight warm gray (#C0BBB9). Cards: 1px border rgba(255,255,255,0.08), 4px radius, 24px padding. Category images desaturated. Spacing between sections: 120px minimum. Grid: 12-col with 24px gutters, max-width 1200px.

3. Use a condensed italic black sans-serif (Tusker Grotesk or Dharma Gothic) at 90-100px uppercase for hero headlines on a warm gray (#DFDCE1) background. Pair with deep navy (#0D0F3B) for nav and text, royal blue (#161AA6) and burnt orange (#F47030) for accent cards. Set body in DM Sans 15px regular. Build an asymmetric 3-zone layout: left text column (40%), center athlete cutout (35% overlapping both zones), right product sidebar (25%). Use 16px border-radius on cards, pill-shaped buttons with 9999px radius, and 8px base spacing unit. Tags use pill outlines with 1px border.

4. Use #F5F5F5 warm off-white background with #0C0B07 near-black text. Single sans-serif font (Inter 400/600) with 56px hero heading at -0.02em tracking. Hero: full-bleed nature photography with product composited at 40% from top, floating data card with backdrop-filter:blur(20px) and rgba(255,255,255,0.85) background positioned right. Feature grid: 2x2 cards with 48px dark rounded-square icon containers (#1A1A1A, 12px radius), 24px card padding, 16px card radius. Product collection: 3-column grid with #F0F0F0 card backgrounds, 16px radius, centered product images. Section spacing 100px, inner padding 24px, 8px base unit throughout.

5. Use a 7-stop monochromatic warm brown palette from #240A05 to #F3DCCB as your entire system — no blues, no grays. Pair an italic transitional serif (Playfair Display) at 48-56px for headlines with DM Sans at 14-16px for body. Set page background to #F3DCCB, use full-bleed close-up beauty photography as hero with absolutely-positioned italic serif labels floating over the image. Create depth with product bottle PNGs rotated 15-30° and placed at page edges with 4px Gaussian blur. Space sections at 80-100px gaps, use pipe-separated category tabs with underline active states in #5F2312.

### Core Lessons
- True editorial luxury design uses mathematical grid systems (golden ratio) to create asymmetric layouts that feel intentional rather than random — the grid is invisible but its harmony is felt
- Restraint IS luxury — one accent color at 2% coverage, one font family, and letting photography do the emotional heavy lifting creates more authority than any gradient or animation ever could.
- A single bold condensed italic typeface at massive scale can carry an entire hero section when paired with a well-composed cutout image that creates diagonal energy across the layout
- Let product photography and data visualizations carry the color story — restraint in the UI palette makes the content feel more premium and trustworthy.
- Derive your entire color palette from your hero photography to create seamless brand-image integration that feels inevitable rather than designed.
