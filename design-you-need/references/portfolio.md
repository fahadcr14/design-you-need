### Color Craft Insights
- **[9]** The cream background (#FFFAE6) is warmer than pure white, which makes the red feel less aggressive and more editorial — a gallery wall effect
- **[9]** The entire color story is warm-shifted — no cool tones anywhere. Even the 'black' is #190200 (warm). This creates an enveloping, analog warmth that pure #000 would destroy.
- **[8]** Each project maintains its own color sub-palette while the portfolio wrapper uses consistent dark + blue, showing systems-level color thinking
- **[8]** The warm off-white (#F2F1EF) vs cool gray background photos creates a subtle temperature contrast that makes the content panel feel like it's floating — a print designer's trick
- **[7]** The warm neutral palette (#887459, #A39C96) references natural building materials — stone, concrete, wood — reinforcing the architecture brand without being literal
- **[7]** The designer maintains completely separate color systems per project without cross-contamination — shows real brand thinking, not just palette swapping
- **[7]** The VISION piece uses selective color (red lips, red lipstick prop) on desaturated photography — a deliberate art direction choice that unifies the crimson palette with the imagery
- **[7]** Each brand has a completely distinct palette strategy matching its market segment — feminine wellness vs stoner-playful vs industrial-masculine
- **[7]** Zero-accent monochrome is extremely hard to pull off — hierarchy must come entirely from size, weight, and spacing. This succeeds because the photography itself provides all visual interest.

## Design Recipes

### Score 9/10 — editorial — Fine art photographer / mixed-media artist portfolio

**Design Recipe:** Use #FFFAE6 warm cream background with #C72920 vermillion red exclusively for headlines and one circular CTA element. Set display type at 120-160px in a geometric sans (Roc Grotesk or Neue Haas Grotesk Bold) with deliberate mixed uppercase/lowercase (lowercase 'i' in otherwise uppercase words). Body text in 12-14px same family regular weight in near-black #090104. Layout on asymmetric grid with 60-100px section gaps, images bleeding into text zones with z-index overlap. All photography treated with warm sepia desaturation. Maximum 3 elements per section.

**Dev Recipe:** Next.js + GSAP ScrollTrigger + CSS Grid with named areas for overlapping layouts. Self-host a geometric sans via @font-face, use clip-path for diamond masks, filter:sepia(0.3) saturate(0.8) for image treatment, and clamp() for fluid display typography scaling.

**Core Lesson:** A single bold typographic decision (mixed-case letterforms) can carry an entire brand identity when paired with extreme color restraint.

**Steal These:**
- Mixed uppercase/lowercase in display type as brand signature
- Single red circle as the only CTA — impossible to miss
- Warm cream (#FFFAE6) instead of white for gallery-paper sophistication
- Text overlapping photography for editorial depth without shadows
- Vertical nav list tucked to the side — minimal but accessible

**Weaknesses:** Small body text (10-12px) may fail accessibility standards — needs size bump, Red-on-cream may not meet WCAG AAA for smaller text sizes, Highly art-directed layout will be challenging to maintain responsively on mobile
