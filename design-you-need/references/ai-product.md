### Color Craft Insights
- **[8]** The warm coral/salmon circuit lines against cool periwinkle create deliberate temperature contrast that guides the eye along connection paths — a sophisticated complementary micro-accent strategy
- **[8]** The palette is almost entirely derived from the hero photography — sage, sky blue, warm earth — creating seamless image-to-UI color continuity
- **[8]** The noise texture on the radio panel surface is key — without it the flat blue-grey would feel dead. It sells the brushed-metal illusion.
- **[8]** The entire palette is warm-shifted — even the grays lean brown. This creates cohesion that most tech sites miss by defaulting to cool neutrals.
- **[8]** The entire palette avoids blue entirely — radical for AI branding — creating instant differentiation through earthy warmth
- **[8]** The entire accent palette stays within a warm analogous range (copper→amber→gold) which is rare discipline — most dark AI sites mix in blue/purple and lose cohesion
- **[8]** The entire palette is derived from a single green hue at varying lightness/saturation — monochromatic in disguise, which is why it feels so cohesive despite light/dark mode switching
- **[8]** The entire palette avoids blue entirely — radical for AI/tech — creating instant brand differentiation through color alone
- **[7]** The green glow on dark backgrounds uses radial-gradient with low opacity (~10-15%) creating ambient light effect without washing out content — this is the signature move
- **[7]** The gradient glow is never centered — it's always offset to create asymmetric light sources that feel like real illumination rather than decorative blobs

## Typography

- **[8] Inter or SF Pro Display — geometric sans + Inter or system sans — humanis**: Single font family at different weights creates clean hierarchy without visual noise, appropriate for enterprise SaaS
- **[8] PP Editorial New or similar high-contras + Inter or similar geometric san**: High-contrast serif for editorial drama paired with clean sans for readability — classic luxury editorial formula
- **[8] Inter or SF Pro Display — geometric sans + Inter or SF Pro Text — humanis**: Single font family at different weights creates cohesion; the LCD digital font on the radio panel provides all the typographic contrast needed
- **[8] Playfair Display or similar transitional + Inter or DM Sans — geometric s**: Serif headlines create editorial authority while geometric sans body ensures readability; italic serif for emphasis words adds elegance.
- **[8] Playfair Display or similar transitional + Inter or DM Sans — geometric s**: Serif italics for emotional/aspirational words, sans for functional text — creates editorial luxury feel
- **[8] Serif — likely Playfair Display or simil + Sans-serif — likely Inter or D**: Serif italic for emotional tagline vs clean sans for functional text creates editorial luxury contrast
- **[8] Instrument Serif or similar modern edito + Inter or DM Sans — geometric s**: Serif headlines convey intelligence and editorial authority while sans body keeps it modern and readable — the italic serif 'be loud' creates emotional emphasis
- **[8] Custom monospaced slab — possibly GT Ame + Sans-serif — likely Inter or s**: Monospaced headings evoke terminal/code culture while sans body ensures readability — bridges tech credibility with accessibility
- **[7] Neue Montreal — geometric sans-serif (co + Neue Montreal Regular — same f**: Single-family system using weight contrast (Regular/Medium/Semibold) creates clean hierarchy without font-pairing complexity
- **[7] Inter or similar geometric sans-serif, p + Inter or system sans-serif**: Single font family at different weights creates clean hierarchy without competing personalities on a data-heavy page

### Typography Effects
- **[8]** Mixed weight within single headline — 'The Future' regular, 'of Interior' italic, 'Architecture' light — creates visual rhythm, achieved via span-level font-style/weight switching
- **[8]** Oversized display type cropped at hero edges — overflow:hidden on container
- **[8]** Underline CTA 'Book a Call' — text-decoration with offset
- **[8]** LCD/digital clock font on radio frequency display — custom @font-face with segment-display typeface
- **[8]** Italic serif on 'Radio Player' title suggesting premium branding
