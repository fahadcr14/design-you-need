# Fitness — Design Playbook

**15 projects analyzed | 10 scored 7+ | Avg: 6.5/10**

## Category Overview

**Aesthetics that work:** warm-minimal (4), dark-luxury (3), bold-expressive (2), dimensional-layered (2), organic-natural (2)
**Color strategy:** neutral-plus-accent (6), analogous (5), dark-theme (4)
**Color mode:** light (8), mixed (4), dark (3)
**Hero patterns:** layered (6), split-50-50 (2), full-bleed (2), centered (2)
**Frameworks:** Next.js or Framer (3), Next.js or React (2), React Native or Flutter (2), React or Next.js (1)
**Animation:** Framer-Motion (4), GSAP (2), Lottie (2)

## Color Rules

- **[8]** Orange used as section markers (small dots), CTA fills, active nav states, and card highlight borders — disciplined but frequent
- **[8]** Lime green reserved strictly for CTAs and section labels — never decorative fill
- **[8]** Burnt orange used only for section labels and subtle highlights — very restrained
- **[7]** Rose-red reserved for primary CTAs and stats bar only — max 10% of page
- **[7]** Orange used sparingly — only on data metrics, links, and gauge elements for emphasis
- **[7]** Neon green used only for actionable elements — CTA, active nav, promo ticker
- **[7]** Gold used sparingly — only on 1-2 key words per section heading and step card accents
- **[7]** Dark green used for CTAs and section containers only — never decorative
- **[7]** Almost no chromatic accent — relies on black/white contrast with photography providing all color
- **[7]** Dark olive reserved for interactive/selected states only; sage-green for progress/data viz

### Color Craft Insights
- **[8]** The warm brown-black (#1D0F05) instead of pure black keeps the dark sections feeling connected to the orange palette — a senior-level color temperature decision
- **[8]** The lime #AFF725 is a genius pick — it literally IS the color of a padel/tennis ball, making the accent feel brand-native rather than arbitrary
- **[8]** The entire palette is derived from skin/earth tones matching the athlete photo — color extraction from hero image creates seamless integration
- **[7]** The entire palette is temperature-locked to warm — no cool blues or grays anywhere, creating an unusually cohesive sensory experience
- **[7]** The sage green hero is the signature move — it avoids the cliché blue-sports-brand trap and creates an earthy, premium feel that differentiates from competitors
- **[7]** The neon green is perfectly calibrated — saturated enough to pop on dark but not so yellow it reads cheap. It's the exact hue fitness brands like Gymshark popularized.
- **[7]** The gold is desaturated enough to feel masculine/premium rather than gaudy — sits perfectly between warm-gray and gold
- **[7]** The entire palette stays within green-olive analogous range — zero hue jumps — creating an unusually cohesive organic feel that most fitness sites miss by adding blue or orange
- **[7]** The palette is essentially monochromatic neutral — all color comes from the photography itself, which is a disciplined editorial choice that lets imagery breathe
- **[7]** The entire palette could be sampled from a single photo of morning light through sage leaves — every color is botanically coherent

## Design Recipes

### Score 8/10 — bold-expressive — Basketball training club with coaching programs and sponsors

**Design Recipe:** Use #DA4C0A orange as primary accent on #1D0F05 warm-black and #F5F0EB warm-white alternating sections. Headlines in bold italic serif (Playfair Display) at 80-120px uppercase with tight letter-spacing, mixed with condensed sans (Barlow Condensed) for body and labels at 11-16px uppercase wide-tracked. Section markers use '// PREFIX' pattern and small orange dots. Stats displayed as oversized numbers (~72px bold) with horizontal rule separators. Pricing cards use white backgrounds with orange left-border accent on featured state. Hero is full-viewport with warm orange gradient overlay, layered player photo, and oversized stroke-outline text anchored to bottom edge.

**Dev Recipe:** Next.js + Tailwind CSS + GSAP ScrollTrigger for scroll animations and marquee. Use CSS grid with named areas for the masonry facilities layout, backdrop-filter for nav, and -webkit-text-stroke for the hero outline text. Framer Motion for page transitions and card hover states.

**Core Lesson:** Mixing an italic high-contrast serif with a condensed industrial sans-serif creates instant sports-editorial authority — the typographic contrast IS the brand.

**Steal These:**
- The /01/ /02/ /03/ numbered badge system scattered across hero as floating stat anchors
- // PREFIX pattern for labels and tags — instant editorial credibility
- Alternating dark/light section rhythm with orange as the connective thread
- Italic serif + condensed sans headline mixing within the same line
- Marquee repeating text strip as a section divider between content blocks

**Weaknesses:** AI-generated coach portraits are uncanny valley — real photography would elevate significantly, Hero composition is busy with scattered stats, player, gradient, AND oversized text competing, Countdown timer section lacks context — what event is being counted down to?

### Score 8/10 — dark-luxury — Premium padel sports club with dining and wellness

**Design Recipe:** Use #040D27 deep navy as full-page background with #FAFAFA text and #AFF725 lime exclusively on CTAs and tags. Set hero headline in condensed uppercase sans (Bebas Neue or Oswald) at 100-120px with tight letter-spacing. Create a rounded-corner container (~20px radius, 2px white/20% opacity border) that frames the hero viewport, then layer a cutout action photo OVER the headline text using z-index stacking (text z:1, image z:2, remaining text z:3). Body text in Inter/DM Sans at 16px #A6B3C7. Cards for amenities use white #FAFAFA backgrounds with 16px radius on the dark canvas. Maintain 80-120px section spacing.

**Dev Recipe:** Build in Next.js or Framer with GSAP for hero parallax. Use CSS custom properties for the 3-color system (#040D27, #FAFAFA, #AFF725). The hero requires absolute positioning with 3 z-index layers — split the headline into two text elements sandwiching the player image. Tailwind with custom config for the navy/lime palette.

**Core Lesson:** Your accent color should be semantically meaningful to the brand — not just visually appealing — and the hero should create physical depth through z-index layering of real imagery with typography.

**Steal These:**
- Lime accent color = literal padel ball color — brand-native accent strategy
- Rounded border frame creating a viewport-within-viewport hero composition
- Player figure breaking through headline text via z-index sandwich
- Avatar cluster with italic community quote as lightweight social proof
- Dotted-line price separators in the dining menu section

**Weaknesses:** Inner page sections (club amenities, events) feel more template-like compared to the hero's craft, The white card sections break the dark immersion somewhat abruptly — could use a transitional treatment, Mobile responsiveness of the hero overlap composition would be very challenging to maintain

### Score 8/10 — dimensional-layered — Rugby athlete performance analytics platform

**Design Recipe:** Use a warm analogous palette anchored in peach (#E3BDA1) and earthy brown (#967664) with near-black (#0D0A0A) text. Set hero headline in a condensed italic black-weight display font (Tusker Grotesk or Dharma Gothic) at 100-120px uppercase with -0.02em tracking. Make one word stroke-outline only. Layer a cutout athlete PNG between headline text (z-index:1) and background gradient (z-index:0), with headline at z-index:2 for some lines and z-index:0 for others. Apply a horizontal linear-gradient from warm grey (#B8B4B0) left to peach (#E3BDA1) right. Use frosted glass cards (backdrop-filter:blur(20px), rgba(228,227,226,0.6)) with 16px border-radius. Keep CTAs as pill-shaped buttons with embedded circular arrow icons.

**Dev Recipe:** Next.js + Tailwind + GSAP ScrollTrigger. Key challenge is the z-index text-image sandwich — split headline into separate div layers, position athlete image absolutely between them. Use backdrop-filter for glass cards, -webkit-text-stroke for outline text, and GSAP for parallax scroll on the athlete and staggered text reveals.

**Core Lesson:** Layering a subject image between text planes creates instant cinematic depth that flat layouts can never achieve

**Steal These:**
- Text-behind-image z-index layering for hero depth
- Outline stroke text mixed with filled text in same headline
- Warm peach-earth gradient derived from hero image skin tones
- Pill CTA with embedded circular arrow icon — two-part button
- Editorial asymmetric stat placement (200K top-right, Nike bottom-right)

**Weaknesses:** Below-fold section feels underdeveloped compared to hero — cards and type hierarchy less considered, Nike partnership badge feels aspirational/fake for a portfolio piece — undermines credibility, Body text contrast on warm gradient areas could be tighter for accessibility
